ODIN'S RECIPE

Odin is a traditional Japanese stew, especially popular in winter. It features ingredients like daikon, boiled eggs, konjac, and fish cakes simmered in a light soy-flavored dashi broth, offering a warm, hearty, and comforting meal with rich umami flavors.

Odin is a classic Japanese one-pot dish, often enjoyed during the colder months. It consists of various ingredients like boiled eggs, daikon radish, konjac, and assorted fish cakes slowly simmered in a light, soy-based dashi broth. Rich in umami and comforting in flavor, Odin is a beloved street food and home-cooked meal across Japan, especially in winter.
